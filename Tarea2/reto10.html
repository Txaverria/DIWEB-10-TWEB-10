The Era of Personal Computers

In the 1970s, the invention of the microprocessor marked the beginning of personal computers (PCs). Companies like Apple and IBM launched models that popularized the use of computers in the home and office. The Apple II and the IBM PC became symbols of a new technological era, where computing became accessible to the general public.

Internet of Things (IoT)

The Internet of Things (IoT) connects everyday devices to the network, allowing automation and remote control of everything from household appliances to security systems. This creates an integrated ecosystem where computers play a central role in information management.

Quantum Computing

Quantum computing promises to solve complex problems much faster than traditional computers. Although it is still in its early stages, it has the potential to transform fields such as cryptography, material simulation, and medical research.
